Chapter 1: Gravity

Introduction

Gravity is the force of attraction that pulls objects toward each other. On Earth, it gives weight to physical objects and causes them to fall toward the ground when dropped.

Newton’s Law of Gravitation

Every object in the universe attracts every other object with a force which is directly proportional to the product of their masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between their centers.

Mathematically: F = G (m₁m₂ / r²)

Acceleration due to Gravity (g)

On the surface of the Earth, all objects experience acceleration due to gravity. The value of g ≈ 9.8 m/s².
